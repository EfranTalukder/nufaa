Finance rooted
in fairness.

Islamic finance forbids interest because it taxes the most vulnerable hardest. Conventional banks built their whole model on that asymmetry. We didn't want to participate.

Nufaa runs on a Wakala (agency) structure. You aren't borrowing — the platform stakes on your behalf, the network yields the value, and your transaction settles. Transparent. Audited. Shared.

The founder

Built by someone
who lived the problem.

Alhassan Bah grew up in Gambia, watching families work three jobs only to lose half of it to interest on a phone bill or a sack of rice. Conventional banking didn't fail by accident — it was built to extract.

Nufaa is his answer. A system where technology, not interest, settles the bill — and where every transaction is grounded in Islamic finance principles. Fairness, here, is operational. Not aspirational.
