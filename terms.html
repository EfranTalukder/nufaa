02

The Wakala Agreement

Nufaa operates strictly on a Sharia-compliant Wakala (agency) model. This is the foundation of everything we do:

  • When you make a purchase through Nufaa, you appoint us as your agent (Wakeel) to acquire and manage Sharia-compliant digital assets on your behalf.
  • No interest (Riba) is ever charged, accumulated, or implied on your account. Your balance can only decrease or remain the same — it will never increase due to charges.
  • The economic relationship is one of agency, not lending. You are not taking out a loan. You are appointing Nufaa to facilitate a transaction using blockchain staking yields.
  • Nufaa earns revenue through a transparent operational fee deducted from staking yields — never from interest on your balance.

This structure has been designed with guidance from Islamic finance principles and is subject to ongoing Sharia compliance review.

05

Staking & Digital Assets

When you deposit funds or make a purchase through Nufaa, the platform acquires Sharia-compliant digital assets and stakes them across enterprise blockchain networks (including but not limited to Cardano, Solana, and Ethereum).

How staking works

  • Staking involves delegating digital assets to support blockchain network validation. In return, the network distributes cryptographic rewards.
  • Nufaa converts these rewards and applies them to reduce your outstanding balance or grow your wallet balance, depending on your account mode.
  • The estimated annual percentage yield (APY) is approximately 4.45%, but this is not guaranteed and fluctuates with network conditions.

Risk disclosure

  • Your principal balance is protected. Staking on Nufaa does not expose you to the risk of losing your deposited funds.
  • Staking yields may decrease during periods of low network activity, meaning your balance reduction may slow — but it will never reverse.
  • Nufaa diversifies staking across multiple blockchains to mitigate concentration risk.
